## Company Introduction

Orbit AI is a technology company building intelligent software for people and organizations who demand more than capability — they demand reliability, honesty, and control.

Founded as Orbit Systems B.V., the company operates under the belief that the most powerful AI is not necessarily the most valuable AI. The most valuable AI is the kind people can actually trust: software that performs consistently, explains itself clearly, protects the data it touches, and works alongside human judgment rather than replacing it.

Orbit develops its workspace and tooling iteratively, driven by real-world use and direct feedback. Every design decision, every engineering choice, and every policy reflects a core conviction: the future of AI depends not only on intelligence, but on trust.

## Why Orbit AI Exists

Most AI tools are built to be impressive. Orbit is built to be dependable.

There is a meaningful difference between software that demonstrates capability in ideal conditions and software that performs reliably in real work environments — under time pressure, with imperfect inputs, across varied tasks, and with real consequences if things go wrong.

Orbit exists to close that gap. The company was founded on the observation that trust and capability are not in tension — they are complementary. AI that earns trust through consistent, honest, transparent behavior is AI that people actually adopt, rely on, and build around.

The team at Orbit believes the AI industry needs more companies willing to say, clearly and publicly: here is what this technology does, here is what it does not do, here is how we handle your data, and here is why you should feel confident using it. Orbit is that company.

## How Orbit AI Approaches Privacy

Privacy is not a compliance checkbox at Orbit. It is an engineering constraint that shapes how the product is designed from the beginning.

**What this means in practice:**

Orbit does not collect user data for advertising. The product does not profile users for commercial purposes. Conversation content, file data, and personal information users bring into the workspace exist to make the product work for the user — not to train models without consent, not to be monetized, and not to be shared with third parties without a clear, legitimate reason.

Orbit is subject to European privacy law, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The company maintains a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for business customers and a clear, plain-language Privacy Policy for individual users.

Users have rights over their data: the right to access it, the right to have it deleted, and the right to understand how it is used. These rights are not buried in fine print — they are actively supported.

The full privacy policy is available at orbitdev.org/Routes/privacy.

## How Orbit AI Approaches Security

Security is treated as a fundamental property of the Orbit platform — not a feature added at the end of development.

Orbit's security approach covers four areas:

**1. Account security**  
Access controls, authentication, and session management are designed to protect user accounts from unauthorized access.

**2. Data security**  
Data transmitted to and from Orbit is encrypted in transit. Data at rest is protected through appropriate technical controls.

**3. Infrastructure security**  
Orbit maintains practices for monitoring, incident response, and vulnerability management. Security considerations are embedded in the development process, not applied after the fact.

**4. AI system integrity**  
Orbit applies responsible practices to the AI capabilities it deploys, including measures to reduce misuse, protect against prompt injection and adversarial inputs, and maintain the integrity of AI-generated outputs.

Orbit maintains a public security policy at orbitdev.org/Routes/security. Responsible disclosure of security concerns is supported and encouraged.

## Responsible AI Principles

Orbit holds itself to a clear set of principles for how AI is developed and deployed.

**Honesty.** Orbit does not present AI capabilities as greater than they are. The product is designed to communicate uncertainty, acknowledge limitations, and provide outputs that inform human judgment rather than replace it.

**Human oversight.** The design of Orbit's AI features keeps people in control. Automated workflows are transparent in what they do. Consequential actions are reviewed by users, not taken silently.

**No harmful applications.** Orbit applies usage policies that prohibit the use of the platform for harm — including harassment, deception, mass surveillance, illegal activity, and the generation of dangerous content. These policies are enforced and publicly available.

**Fairness.** Orbit is attentive to bias in AI outputs and committed to improving quality and fairness across the diverse range of users and use cases the platform serves.

**Accountability.** When something goes wrong — a bug, a privacy issue, a security incident, a misleading output — Orbit addresses it directly and communicates clearly. Accountability is not an optional extra.

The full AI usage policy is available at orbitdev.org/Routes/ai-usage.

## Our Technology and Engineering Philosophy

Orbit approaches software engineering with a bias toward quality, composability, and sustainable development.

**Quality over speed.** Orbit ships incrementally and carefully. Version 0.0.20 reflects a product in active, honest development — not a finished product oversold as complete. Features are added when they are ready, not to meet marketing schedules.

**Composability.** The Orbit workspace connects AI capabilities, automation, file handling, and collaboration in a way that allows users to build their own workflows. The architecture favors flexibility over lock-in.

**Reliability as a first-class concern.** Uptime, consistent performance, and graceful handling of errors are engineering goals, not aspirations. The product is designed to be dependable when work depends on it.

**Privacy by design.** Privacy decisions are made at the architectural level. Data minimization, access controls, and user control are built into the system, not applied as a layer on top.

**Honest versioning.** Orbit's version numbers reflect actual development stage. The current public release at 0.0.20 is an early-stage product with real capabilities and known limitations. Users are not misled about where the product stands.

## Our Approach to Innovation

Orbit approaches innovation with discipline.

New capabilities are explored carefully before deployment. Features that introduce meaningful risks — to privacy, to security, to user trust — are evaluated against those risks before shipping. Speed to market is never prioritized over user safety or product integrity.

Orbit monitors the development of AI capabilities broadly and incorporates advances that genuinely serve users — not for novelty, but for utility. The company's interest is in building the best possible workspace, not in being first to announce every new model or technique.

Innovation at Orbit is also about process: improving reliability, expanding access, reducing friction, and making capable technology accessible to people who are not AI specialists.

## Founder Story

Orbit AI was founded as Orbit Systems B.V. in the Netherlands.

The company emerged from a direct observation: existing AI tools were becoming increasingly capable, but they were not becoming more trustworthy. Capability without accountability creates tools that are powerful but not dependable — impressive in demos but difficult to rely on in serious work.

The founding of Orbit was motivated by a specific set of convictions: that AI software should be honest about what it can and cannot do, that privacy must be engineered into products from the start, that security cannot be an afterthought, and that the people who build AI tools should be willing to stand publicly behind the choices they make.

The Orbit product has been developed iteratively from early versions through the current public release at v0.0.20. The company operates lean, is based in the Netherlands under Dutch and European regulatory frameworks, and publishes its development openly.

Orbit is a company in the early stages of something it intends to build with care over a long period of time. The founders chose to build slowly and correctly over building quickly and carelessly.
