Acceptable Use Policy

Rules for acceptable use of Orbit AI, automation, coding, browser, API, and workflow features.

Effective: July 6, 2026Orbit Systems B.V.Contact: support@orbitdev.org
This page is provided for product transparency and general compliance information. Enterprise customers may have additional or superseding terms in a signed order form, DPA, or service agreement.

001 / Lawful Use

Use Orbit only for lawful, authorized, and ethical purposes. You are responsible for complying with laws, platform terms, contracts, and data rights that apply to your activity.

002 / No Harmful Activity

Do not use Orbit to build, deploy, or assist malware, phishing, credential theft, exploit chains, botnets, spam systems, or unauthorized access.

003 / No Rights Violations

Do not upload, generate, scrape, or distribute content that violates privacy, publicity, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or contractual rights.

004 / No Deception

Do not use Orbit to impersonate people or organizations, misrepresent identity, create fraudulent documents, manipulate reviews, or run deceptive campaigns.

005 / No Harassment or Abuse

Do not use Orbit to threaten, harass, exploit, dox, stalk, or target people or protected groups.

006 / Regulated Uses

Do not use Orbit as the sole decision system for medical, legal, financial, employment, housing, insurance, education, or safety-critical outcomes.

007 / Automation Limits

Automated browsing, messaging, scraping, testing, and workflow execution must respect rate limits, consent, robots rules, and third-party service terms.

008 / Security Research

Security testing must be authorized, scoped, non-destructive, and must avoid accessing or exposing data that is not yours.

009 / Account Integrity

Do not share accounts in ways that bypass plan limits, resell access without permission, evade usage restrictions, or interfere with service operations.

010 / Enforcement

Orbit may throttle, block, suspend, terminate, remove content, or report activity when needed to protect users, infrastructure, rights holders, or legal compliance.
